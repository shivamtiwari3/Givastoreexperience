Part 1

The Experience, As It Happened

6:20 PM. GIVA Store, Hyderabad Airport. A mangalsutra catches the customer's eye.

GIVA Store, Hyderabad Airport
Gold Komal Lab Grown Diamond Mangalsutra

The store is clean, well-lit, thoughtfully designed. A mangalsutra catches the customer's eye: the Gold Komal Lab Grown Diamond Mangalsutra, sitting on a burgundy velvet bust. It's delicate. The pendant has a diamond X motif with hanging chains. It feels like the right price band. The customer wants to buy it, but not alone. Their partner isn't there.

So begins the attempt to bring the product into the digital world.

Attempt 1: Text Search on GIVA App
Searched the product name. Got 77 results. Then 100. None were the piece.
The sales associate provided the product name. The customer typed "Gold Komal Lab Grown Diamond Mangalsutra" into the GIVA app's search bar. The app returned 77 results, then 100 on a broader search. Studs, rings, pendants. None of them the mangalsutra they were holding.
Product not found
Attempt 2: SKU Search
Typed GDLNE0113. Zero results.
The associate pointed to the tag. It read: GDLNE0113. Clear barcode. Clean print. The customer typed it into the search bar. "No results found for GDLNE0113." The product physically in their hand, tagged, barcoded, priced at ₹46,964 on the store's POS, returns zero results.
Zero results for valid SKU
Attempt 3: Google Lens
Google knows it's GIVA. But can't find the product.
The associate suggests: "Scan it on Google, it'll take you to our site." Google Lens correctly identifies the brand. It surfaces similar mangalsutras at ₹43,772 and ₹54,213, but not this piece. The customer is now looking at competing products within the GIVA catalog.
Brand identified, product not matched
Attempt 4: POS Lookup (Internal)
The product exists perfectly inside the staff system.
The associate turns the store laptop around. There it is: Gold Komal Lab Grown Diamond Mangalsutra. Gold weight: 2.4g. Gold price: ₹21,170. Making charges: ₹20,350. Total: ₹46,964. Every data point, perfectly structured, sitting in GIVA's internal POS. The product exists. The data is rich. The customer just can't see any of it.
Data exists but is invisible to customers

Part 2: Business Requirements

Who Is This Customer, Really?

Before jumping to fixes, it's worth understanding who was standing in that store. This isn't an edge case. This is GIVA's core buyer.

Primary Persona
The Considerate Buyer
28 to 38, urban, emotionally invested in the purchase
She's buying a mangalsutra, an emotionally loaded purchase. The decision involves someone else: a partner, a mother, a sister. She will not buy something this personal on the spot without consultation.

She discovers in-store, where the tactile experience matters. But she decides online or in conversation. She'll photograph it, share it on WhatsApp, check reviews, maybe sleep on it. This is not a weakness in her buying behavior. It IS her buying behavior. Any brand that doesn't accommodate this loop loses her at the "share it" step.
Secondary Persona
The Gift Buyer
30 to 45, 40 minutes before a flight, wants something meaningful
He walked into the airport store because he has 40 minutes and wants to buy something meaningful for his wife. He liked the piece. But ₹46,964 is not a walk-away spend.

He wants to verify the price online, check exchange policy, maybe save it to buy when he lands and his wife can confirm. He needs a product link. He cannot get one. He boards the flight. The purchase never happens.
Tertiary Persona
The Researcher
25 to 35, found GIVA on Instagram, validating in-store
She found GIVA on Instagram. She walks into the airport store, sees the exact piece she imagined. She wants to find the product on Google, read the lab-grown diamond certification, share it with her sister.

She scans with Google Lens. Gets near-matches. Leaves without a product link. By the time she lands, the emotional peak has passed.
The Core Insight

The Emotional Purchase Window

In jewellery retail, there is a window, typically 2 to 48 hours, during which the emotional connection to a piece is strongest. The customer saw it, touched it, imagined wearing it. That window is the highest-converting moment in the entire purchase journey.

What GIVA needs in that window is frictionless digital handoff: the ability to move the experience from physical to digital without any gap, so the emotional peak carries through to purchase.

What GIVA has instead is a dead end. The customer tries every channel available to them and hits a wall. By the time they find the product, if they ever do, the emotional peak has cooled. The purchase that was nearly certain becomes uncertain.

This is not a minor UX inconvenience. This is a revenue leak at the highest-intent moment in the customer journey.

Diagnosis

The Underlying Business Problem

GIVA has built excellent internal infrastructure. The POS system has structured, detailed product data: weight, pricing components, certification, GST breakdowns. This is the hard part. Most jewellery brands can't do this cleanly. GIVA can.

The gap is entirely in the last mile, specifically the sync between internal catalog and customer-facing discovery surfaces. Three surfaces are broken:

Surface 1: Broken
GIVA App Search
SKUs are not indexed as searchable fields. Product names return broad category results rather than exact matches. The search experience behaves like a category browser, not a product finder.
Surface 2: Broken
Google Shopping / Lens
Product pages are either not submitted to Google Merchant Center or the feed is incomplete. Google Lens cannot match a photographed product to a specific GIVA page. It can only identify the brand.
Surface 3: Missing
Physical-to-Digital Bridge
The tag has a barcode (for POS) but no QR code for customer use. No mechanism for a customer to self-serve a digital connection to the product they're holding. Everything depends on the associate.

Part 3: Product Requirements

User Stories

Five stories that capture every broken moment in the journey and what "done" looks like.

US-01
The SKU Search
As a customer standing in a GIVA store, I want to type the SKU from the product tag into the GIVA app and be taken directly to that product page, so that I can save it, share it, and buy it later without depending on the sales associate.

Acceptance Criteria

  • Typing any valid SKU returns the exact product as the first result
  • Result loads within 2 seconds
  • Product page has a "Save to Wishlist" option that does not require login
Current state: Zero results returned for GDLNE0113.
US-02
The QR Scan
As a customer who doesn't know the product name or SKU, I want to point my phone camera at the tag and be taken to the product page, so I can browse without needing to ask for help.

Acceptance Criteria

  • Every product tag has a QR code
  • QR code deep-links to giva.co/products/[SKU]
  • Product page loads correctly on mobile with a share button above the fold
Current state: No QR code on any product tag. Barcode requires a dedicated scanner app.
US-03
The Partner Share
As a customer who wants to consult my partner before buying, I want to share the exact product with image, description, and price via WhatsApp in one tap, so we can decide together even if I'm at the airport.

Acceptance Criteria

  • Product page has a WhatsApp share button pre-populating name, image, price, and URL
  • Shared link opens to a mobile-optimized product page
  • Page includes "Buy Online" and "Find in Store" CTAs
Current state: No product page the customer can navigate to. No share mechanism.
US-04
The Associate Assist
As a GIVA sales associate, I want to scan a product on the POS and send its product link directly to a customer's WhatsApp, so warm leads don't go cold when the customer walks out.

Acceptance Criteria

  • POS has a "Send to Customer" button on every product view
  • Associate enters phone number, taps send
  • Customer receives WhatsApp message with product card within 30 seconds
  • Each send is logged as a lead against the store location
Current state: Associates have no mechanism to do this.
US-05
The Google Lens Discovery
As a customer who saw a GIVA piece at a friend's home or event, I want to scan it with my phone and be taken to the exact product page, so I can buy it without knowing the name.

Acceptance Criteria

  • Google Lens surfaces the correct product page in the top 3 results
  • Product pages have Product schema markup (JSON-LD)
  • Google Merchant Center feed is current and includes all active SKUs
Current state: Google Lens identifies the brand but cannot surface the specific product.
The Roadmap

Four Fixes. Three Weeks. Two Engineers.

Modern AI-assisted engineering makes these fixes dramatically faster than traditional timelines. Here's the path.

Fix 1: SKU Search Indexing

Days, not weeks

The GIVA app's search layer (likely Algolia, Elasticsearch, or Shopify-native) doesn't include SKU as a searchable field in its index schema.

This is not a feature build. It's a configuration fix followed by a data sync. Identify the search index config, add SKU to searchable fields, re-index, verify with sample SKUs, deploy.

Done looks like: Type GDLNE0113 into the GIVA app and Gold Komal Lab Grown Diamond Mangalsutra appears as result #1.

Fix 2: QR Codes on Tags

Days, operationally

URL structure: giva.co/p/GDLNE0113 redirects to the full product page. Build the redirect middleware (under an hour), then update the tag print template with QR code. For existing inventory, a sticker run works.

Product page additions: WhatsApp share button, wishlist save (no login required), "Find in Store" CTA.

Done looks like: Customer points camera at tag, lands on product page, shares to WhatsApp in one tap.

Fix 3: WhatsApp Lead Capture

1 to 2 weeks

Highest-ROI feature in this document. Every store visit that doesn't convert in-person is a lead. Right now, GIVA captures none of them.

WhatsApp Business API + a "Send to Customer" button on the POS. Associate enters phone number, customer gets WhatsApp message with product card, GIVA has a logged, trackable lead.

Non-engineering dependency: WhatsApp template approval takes 3 to 5 business days. Apply immediately.

Done looks like: Associate scans, enters number, customer gets product card on WhatsApp, lead logged.

Fix 4: Google Shopping Feed

1 to 2 weeks

Layer A: Automated product feed export to Google Merchant Center, mapping SKU, name, price, image, availability, and GTIN. Google Shopping listings appear within days.

Layer B: JSON-LD structured data on every product page: name, SKU, brand, image, offers. One-time template change that applies across all pages.

Done looks like: Google Lens scan of any GIVA piece surfaces the GIVA product page in results.

What's not broken: Your internal data. Your POS has everything: weight, price components, certification, SKU. The product exists perfectly in your system. It just doesn't reach the customer.
